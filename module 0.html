0.1.1 · Three Paradigm Shifts from Traditional ICT to GenAI

Generative AI Is Not Just Another Educational Technology

Drawing on the journal article that grounds this workshop, the move from traditional ICT (databases, LMSs, search engines) to GenAI represents not an incremental upgrade but three fundamental paradigm shifts. Each shift reshapes what teachers do — and what teachers need to know.

👇 Click each tab to explore one shift. Read both columns side-by-side to feel the contrast.
TRADITIONAL ICT
A Static Epistemology
Traditional ICT stores, organises, delivers, and displays pre-existing knowledge through tools such as databases, search engines, and learning management platforms. It does not produce new knowledge but only facilitates access to what already exists.
GENAI
A Generative Epistemology
GenAI possesses a generative epistemology, which endows it with disruptive potential to autonomously produce original content, pedagogical solutions, and research insights through emergent algorithmic processes.
→ What this means for you: Knowledge is no longer just retrieved; it is actively co-produced. Teachers now share authorship of knowledge with their tools.
TRADITIONAL ICT
Teacher-Prescribed Adoption
By integrating into regular educational activities, ICTs are mostly used to provide operational-level convenience rather than to generate cognitive insights. Humans conduct all thinking — adaptive learning, information synthesis, and error correction.
GENAI
Learner-Directed Engagement
Through a rapid ideation–feedback–iteration process, GenAI reconfigures some once-human-exclusive traits by augmenting learners' creativity and critical thinking, and prompting them to critically evaluate generated content.
→ What this means for you: Students don't just receive instruction — they direct dialogue with intelligent tools. Critical evaluation becomes a core student skill, not just a teacher's role.
TRADITIONAL ICT
Leaders and Instructors
Although ICTs are often seen as additional assistive tools, teachers lead educational design in most cases. Teachers integrate ICTs into existing pedagogical frameworks as a supplementary tool to enhance delivery, efficiency, and access.
GENAI
Coaches and Mediators
GenAI redefines educational missions, theories, and practices. University teachers must now be prepared to address novel ethical, pedagogical, and technological challenges brought by AI and to equip students for a future shaped by human-AI collaboration.
→ What this means for you: Your value moves from "deliverer of content" toward "designer of meaningful, ethical, AI-rich learning experiences" — and this is precisely what the four-dimension framework helps you build.
🔄 THE BIG-PICTURE IMPLICATION

0.2.2 · How to Use the Workshop & Platform

Five Habits That Make This Work

📊

Re-take the assessment often

The post-test isn't a one-shot exam. Take it as often as you can — it's a feedback loop that calibrates what you're learning.

🧠

Both consolidation AND practice

Declarative learning (knowing) and procedural learning (doing) together drive long-term memory and real classroom application.

⏱️

10 minutes a day, not 7 hours once

Don't cram. Spaced learning consistently beats marathon sessions. Each module breaks naturally into short sittings.

🎯

Focus, then break

Concentrate fully when learning new concepts — and then take a real break before the next session. Both halves matter.

🌱 THE MOST IMPORTANT MINDSET

Stay reflexive — and self-encouraging

"Basic" doesn't mean "bad". "Advanced" doesn't mean "good". The levels are a reference and guidance — a way to see where you are and what could come next, not a verdict on your worth as an educator.

"The framework is a map, not a measuring stick. You're allowed to be at Basic in one dimension and Advanced in another — that's normal, not a flaw."

2️⃣ Reading the Radar Charts

Two radar charts are the heart of your report. Each has four axes — one for each dimension of the framework — and shows two overlaid shapes:

  • Solid orange shape = your current ability level
  • Dashed orange shape = your last assessment (so you can see growth)

The further the shape extends toward an axis edge, the higher you scored on that dimension.

💡 WHAT TO LOOK FOR
  • Asymmetry: A lopsided shape means you're stronger in some dimensions than others — totally normal.
  • Movement: Compare solid vs dashed. Even small outward shifts are real growth.
  • Difference between the two charts: See Step 3 for what "Teacher stance" vs "Student stance" really means.

3️⃣ Why Two Charts? The Two Perspectives

A core idea of this framework: every dimension has two perspectives, and being strong in one doesn't guarantee strength in the other.

📍 Teacher stance = Teacher's own literacy
Your personal capacity to use, evaluate, and collaborate with GenAI in your work.
📍 Student stance = Empowering students' literacy
Your pedagogical capacity to guide students' responsible, critical, and creative engagement with GenAI.
💡 KEY INSIGHT
  • If your two radar charts look very different, that's important information — not a flaw.
  • A common pattern: stronger in personal use, weaker in teaching it. The reverse also happens.
  • The workshop modules address both stances explicitly in every dimension.

4️⃣ Each Dimension Gets Two Pairs of Strength + Growth

Below the radar charts, the report breaks down every dimension into a structured analysis. For each dimension you'll see two blocks — one for Teacher stance, one for Student stance — and each block contains:

✓ STRENGTH
A statement describing what you currently can do at your level. This isn't praise — it's a precise description of your competency.
→ AREAS OF IMPROVEMENT
A statement of the next level up — what to aim for. If you're already Advanced, this becomes "Keep up the great work!"
💡 HOW TO USE THIS
  • Read the strength + improvement together — they're a pair, not separate ratings.
  • The improvement statement tells you exactly which workshop module addresses your next step.
  • Use this as a checklist when you re-take the assessment to see what's shifted.

5️⃣ Personalised Learning Recommendations

At the bottom of the report, the platform suggests three resources from the wider PD pool, matched to your specific gaps. Each recommendation has a match score from 0 to 100.

💡 INTERPRETING MATCH SCORES
  • 90–100: Highly aligned with your improvement areas — start here.
  • 50–89: Relevant adjacent skill — useful after the top recommendation.
  • Below 50: Optional / general background — only if you have time.

Recommendations refresh every time you re-take the assessment, so they evolve with your growth.
